Heinrich Th Heberlein Jr Violin, Germany, 1934

About this item

Paul Knorr, born in 1882, studied violinmaking with Dolling. Working in Berlin and Breslau, he established himself in the Markneukirchen workshop of F & R Enders in 1918. Starting in 1921, Knorr became and independent maker. Attaining mastery of his craft, Knorr was highly respected for wood and material selection. He was also adept at following the patterns, approach, and varnishing techniques of the Cremonese masters.

Tetsuo Matsuda Violin, Barrington, IL 2002

About this item

Tetsuo Matsuda completed his studies at the International Violinmaking School in Cremona Italy in 1981 before moving to Chicago and beginning his career at William Harris Lee in 1983. Matsuda has won silver medals at the Antonio Stradivari International Violin Makers Competition as well as gold medals at the Violin Society of America's International Competition.

About this item

Masa Inokuchi immigrated to Canada in 1968 and established his shop in Toronto. Using only the finest North American tonewood that has aged more than 25 years, Masa and son Miki have produced some of the most sought after contemporary violins and violin family instruments. Perfectly balanced, this example of the master's work provides a smooth, crisp tone that both the listener and the player should find delightful.
